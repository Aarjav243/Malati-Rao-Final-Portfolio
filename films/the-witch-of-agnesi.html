The Witch of Agnesi

A short fiction about Maria Gaetana Agnesi, Italian mathematician

Maria Gaetana Agnesi (1718–1799) was one of the first women in the Western world to achieve recognition as a mathematician. She is best known for her work on a cubic curve later translated into English as the "Witch of Agnesi" — a mistranslation of the Italian versiera (turning curve) that persisted through history.

The Witch of Agnesi is a short fiction film made during Malati Rao's MFA studies at Temple University, Philadelphia. It was the first of her explorations into the intersection of women, knowledge, and institutional power — themes that would recur across her documentary work.
