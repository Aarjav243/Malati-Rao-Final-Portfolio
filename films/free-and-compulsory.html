Free and Compulsory

A documentary on the Right to Education in India

In 2009, India passed the Right to Education Act — a landmark legislation promising free and compulsory education for every child between the ages of six and fourteen. Free and Compulsory asks what that promise has meant in practice, traveling across communities to examine the gap between legislative intent and lived reality.

The film meets children, teachers, parents, and administrators grappling with infrastructure, attendance, quality, and the complex social forces that keep children out of classrooms. Produced by Films Division and PSBT, the film was broadcast nationally.
