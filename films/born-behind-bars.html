Born Behind Bars

A documentary about children growing up inside Indian prisons

In India, children below the age of six can live inside prison with an incarcerated mother. Born Behind Bars enters this hidden world — following children who know no other home, whose first years unfold within prison walls, alongside mothers serving sentences of varying lengths and for varying crimes.

The film asks what it means to grow up in a place defined by confinement, and what the state owes to children who have committed no crime. It is a portrait of resilience, love, and an extraordinary childhood — and a quiet indictment of institutional indifference.

Born Behind Bars was awarded and screened at several national and international film festivals and broadcast on national television.
