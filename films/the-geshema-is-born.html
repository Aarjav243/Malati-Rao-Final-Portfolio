The Geshema is Born

Tibetan Buddhist nuns and the pursuit of the highest academic distinction in Tibetan Buddhism

For centuries, Tibetan Buddhist monks have held the Geshe degree — the equivalent of a doctorate in Buddhist philosophy. Women were barred from this pursuit. The Geshema is Born documents the historic moment when twenty Tibetan Buddhist nuns sat their final examinations for the Geshema degree, breaking a barrier that had stood for centuries.

Shot over three years across monastic communities in India, the film follows these women as they navigate the intellectual rigors of the examination, the weight of their history, and what their achievement means for Tibetan Buddhism and for women everywhere. The Dalai Lama had granted permission for this qualification in 2013; the first class completed their exams in 2016.

The film was broadcast on KVRO Dutch Public Broadcasting, acquired by Cineplex (Taiwan) and Asian Documentaries (Japan), and screened internationally.
