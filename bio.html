Biography

Malati Rao is an award-winning documentary filmmaker and educator based in Pune, India. Over two decades she has made films that document lives at the margins — children growing up inside prisons, Tibetan Buddhist nuns seeking higher education, the artisans keeping India's craft traditions alive, and individuals navigating questions of identity, rights, and belonging.

Her films have been broadcast on national and international television and screened at festivals across South Korea, Japan, Italy, Spain, the United Kingdom, the United States, and India. She won Best Woman Director at the Bangkok International Documentary Awards 2020 for The Geshema is Born, a 56-minute film about Tibetan Buddhist nuns and their pursuit of the Geshema degree — the highest academic distinction in Tibetan Buddhism.

Malati's work has been developed at leading international documentary labs including Sheffield DocFest, DocEdge Kolkata, EsoDoc (European Social Documentary), and Tokyo Docs. Her films have been acquired by KVRO Dutch Public Broadcasting (Netherlands), Cineplex (Taiwan), and Asian Documentaries (Japan).

She is currently directing two feature-length documentaries: Funeral in my Brain, a series on mental health produced by Drishyam Films, India; and Hopes and Fears, about the plea for marriage equality in India, produced by Roo Films.

Malati holds an MFA in Film from Temple University, Philadelphia, an MA in Mass Communication from Jamia Millia Islamia, Delhi, and a BA in English Literature from St Stephen's College, Delhi University. She is currently Professor of Practice in Film and Television Management at the School of Communication, FLAME University, Pune.
