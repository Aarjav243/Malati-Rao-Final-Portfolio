Practice

Film Work

Malati Rao has been making documentaries for the last decade, several of which have been broadcast on public television. Handmade in India, on India’s craft traditions and Born Behind Bars, about children growing up in prison, have been awarded and screened at several film festivals. She won Best Woman Director at the Bangkok International Documentary Awards in 2020 for The Geshema is Born. Malati holds an MFA degree in Film and Media Arts from Temple University, USA, and an MA in Mass Communication from Jamia Millia Islamia, Delhi. She teaches Film at FLAME University, Pune India.

Development

Work developed at leading labs including Sheffield DocFest, DocEdge Kolkata, EsoDoc (European Social Documentary), and Tokyo Docs. Films acquired by KVRO Dutch Public Broadcasting (Netherlands), Cineplex (Taiwan), and Asian Documentaries (Japan).

Current Projects

Currently directing Hopes and Fears, about the plea for marriage equality in India, produced by Roo Films.
